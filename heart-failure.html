Heart Failure Prediction

View Project

Keywords: Machine Learning, Heart Failure Prediction, Artificial Intelligence, SVM, Naive Bayes, ANN, Optuna, Hyper-parameter tuning, SMOTE, Logistic Regression, K-Nearest Neighbor, CatBoost, Model Evaluation

In my current project, "Heart Failure Prediction," initiated in May 2024 as part of an Artificial Intelligence course, I contributed to base-model implementation and hyper-parameter tuning, as well as constructing the final report. The project focuses on identifying the optimal model for predicting heart failure by implementing various machine learning models and performing extensive hyper-parameter tuning.

We applied SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique) to address class imbalance in the dataset, ensuring a more accurate prediction model. The models evaluated included Naive Bayes, SVM, Logistic Regression, K-Nearest Neighbor, CatBoost, and Artificial Neural Networks (ANN). To fine-tune these models, we utilized Optuna, a hyper-parameter optimization framework, which significantly enhanced model performance.

Through this rigorous approach, we increased the prediction accuracy to approximately 90%, demonstrating the effectiveness of combining advanced machine learning techniques and hyper-parameter tuning in medical data analysis.
